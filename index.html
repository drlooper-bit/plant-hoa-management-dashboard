Community Overview

Operational Profile

  • Plantation at Leesburg is a 55+ gated community of approximately 2,820 homes on 1,757 acres, with two 18-hole golf courses, clubhouses, pools, tennis/pickleball courts, and Steve's Bar & Grill.
  • Board leadership includes President Russ Roberson, Vice President Jerry Tucker, Secretary/Treasurer Geoff Alpert, Directors Nancy Sheahan and Pam Rudolph, HOA Manager Dennis Bard, and Assistant Manager Caren Petranovic.
  • The HOA retains member-grown delinquency pressure, contracting differences, exposed LLC structural risks, and statute-level compliance gaps that require deliberate remediation.

Bylaw Snapshot

Governance Power

  • Class B votes: Declarant gets 3 votes per lot + 1 per unbuilt plan unit until 95% turnover.
  • Officers are unpaid.
  • Special meetings can be called by President, board majority, or 10% of all Members.

Turnaround Plan

Objective

  • Move the community from structural repair mode to sustainable operations without destabilizing assessments or reserve trajectories.
  • Protect property values and marketability by resolving litigation, restoring audit transparency, and removing disclosure handicaps.

Priority Workstreams

  • 1. Audit Completion: close 2023/2024 audits, release statements, and restore financial credibility with owners and lenders.
  • 2. Litigation Discipline: contain receivership and special-assessment exposure, then replace friction with a binding compliance roadmap.
  • 3. Delinquency Containment: collect the ~$222,000 internal delinquency balance with explicit rules for severely delinquent accounts and collection counsel oversight.
  • 4. Contract Transparency: publish all material contracts, bids, and vendor packages as required by statute and member expectations.
  • 5. Infrastructure Governance: enforce the 10-Year Asset Management Plan firewalls and restrict golf liabilities from penetrating residential reserve funding.
  • 6. Revenue Stabilization: evaluate Steve’s Bar & Grill renewal, golf pricing, Brightview contract economics, and irrigated-grass maintenance assumptions against break-even reality.

OwnerImpact

  • Baseline residential maintenance assessment is $165/month, with higher tier historic obligations at $180/month; $15/month is tied to golf operations.
  • Turnaround priorities must preserve the existing reserve recovery path while eliminating avoidable legal and contracting surprises that drive hidden costs.
