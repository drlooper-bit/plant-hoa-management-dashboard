Why a Recall of the Current HOA Board Is Necessary

Active Process

Homeowners at The Plantation at Leesburg deserve transparent, accountable, and competent leadership — especially when our financial security and community standards are at stake.

According to the data and documentation presented on this HOA Irregularities Hub:

Reserve Fund Discrepancies: Bank statements reveal a $135,000 decrease in reserves, while the board claimed a $70,000 increase. This contradiction raises serious questions about financial reporting and stewardship. The board has allowed him to remain in his position, raising profound concerns about judgment, safety, and accountability.

Taken together, these issues demonstrate a clear and ongoing pattern of poor governance, lack of transparency, and failure to protect homeowner interests. Under Florida Statute § 720.303(10), we have the right to recall board members with or without cause when such failures occur.

This dashboard serves as a central resource to document these concerns, gauge community support, and organize a responsible transition to new leadership that prioritizes fiscal responsibility, compliance, and the best interests of our 2,820-home community.

📋 The Step-by-Step Transition Plan

We are following a careful, phased approach based on the statute and official guidance:

Phase 1: Digital Straw Poll (Current Phase)

Tracking resident interest here on the dashboard. No physical ballots or formal action will begin until we confirm sufficient support. One-time voting codes will be supplied to each resident via a sealed envelope containing their unique code, distributed by the village captain volunteer.

Phase 2: Volunteer & Block Captain Organization (30 days)

For a community of 2,820 homes, a successful recall requires at least 1,411 valid signatures. Over the next 30 days we will recruit block captains and confirm household coverage so that Phase 3 can launch with full distribution capacity.

Phase 3: 120-Day Signature Collection

Once launched, we will circulate legally compliant Written Recall Agreements aligned with DBPR guidelines. Ballots must be properly completed and signed by authorized parcel owners.

Phase 4: Official Statutory Service

Completed agreements will be served on the board. The board has exactly 5 full business days to certify the recall. If rejected, they must promptly file for arbitration with the Division of Florida Condominiums, Timeshares, and Mobile Homes.

Distribution Workflow for Village Captains

  1. Village captain receives a sealed pack of 2,820 identical envelopes, each containing a unique 8-character voting code.
  2. Captain creates a private offline ledger matching each envelope serial number to a household address.
  3. One envelope is hand-delivered or mailed to each household with a short instruction sheet.
  4. Household opens the envelope, enters the code on this dashboard tab, and submits their vote.
  5. The system marks the code as used — it cannot be voted again. The captain updates their offline ledger.
